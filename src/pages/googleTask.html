Interviews

Having no experience with pet adoption, I needed to talk to to someone who knew what the problem is

I interviewed two experts to better understand what are the challenges that come with the adoption of rescued animals. I run two 30 mins interviews with Bizhou and Kate. The former started and runs an adoption service for foster dogs in Shangai, the latter is a volounteer at the cat shelter in Archway, London.

Key learning

  • People often think they know what they want but rarely really do. The best way to match a pet is to really understand the adopter’s lifestyle to avoid picking what wrongly they think they want.
  • Educating the new owners is sometimes very important. If they never owned an animal before, or decide to take a difficult pet it is important they get support to make sure pet and adopter has a good life together.
  • Meeting the right dog is like dating, you never really know who’s going to have that special something.
  • Helping to adopt overlooked animals is all down to finding the right person and really being careful at not overselling his qualities.

The problem

Defining the problem space

Overlooked animals (middle-age or senior, special needs, quiet or nervous, dark/black coloured) take about four times longer to get adopted that the average pet, according to the adoption database by Petfinder.com.

This inspired me to create a service with an emphasis on the 'overlooked' animals and with the goal of helping them find their forever home.

Another problem I see with many adoption websites is that the experience of adopting a pet often feels like a shopping experience. Instead, in order to create a perfect match for both adopter and pet, I would like my concept to rely on the data collected from adopters, combined with the knowledge of the shelter staff.

My objective

Giving an equal chance to all pets to find their forever homes

By creating a service which is centered around the pet’s needs and the adopter's wishes I hope to give all pets equal chance to adoption. To support this concept the service is based on three key principles:

1

Instead of having adopters choose and animals, pets are matched to a potential owner based on needs, lifestyle etc.

2

Pet and adopter are matched one at a time. This should linit any bias against the pet and ensure that the adopter is “open” to a slightly different pet then initially expected.

3

The match is made possible thanks to the combination of insights on the adopter, gathered through the questionnaire, and the knowledge of the shelter staff.

Design #2: Pet profile

A truthfull and tranparent profile

The pet profile brings together two of the key aspects of the service, a personal 1-to-1 match and the opinion of an expert.

A pet with a story to tell

Animals are often overlooked for the silliest of reasons. A simple defect, or the wrong fur color can put a pet at the bottom of the list of a potential adopter.
Overly positive descriptions also can cause problem when they oversell the animal’s personality. 

I wanted to overcome these issues by providing clear and transparent information about the pet. The animal’s description highlights the good but also the negative aspects that come with this particular animal.

An expert’s view on your potential petmate

The idea of a hand picked match is re-enforced with the addition of information from the person doing the match. The user can then directly contact the match maker to ask for more information.

Design #3: Questionnaire

A simple but pleasant questionnaire

The questionnaire is a key aspect of the match making, it is how the people doing the matching get their information about adopters.
